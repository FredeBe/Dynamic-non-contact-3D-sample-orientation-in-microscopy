SPIM has become the technique of choice for in vivo imaging in developmental biology Huisken 2004 Weber 2014. In contrast to most light microscopy techniques, SPIM provides an axis of rotation about which the sample is rotated to take several views. However, a single rotational-axis system cannot give the full three-dimensional freedom of rotation. Therefore, not all parts of the sample can be turned in front of the detection objective and are accessible, e.g. for photo-manipulation.
