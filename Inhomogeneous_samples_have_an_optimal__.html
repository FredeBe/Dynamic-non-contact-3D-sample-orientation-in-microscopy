Inhomogeneous samples have an optimal orientation when studied by light microscopy. For example in developmental biology highly scattering tissues such as pigments, the eyes or the yolk can obscure the organ or tissue of interest. To align the sample in its preferred orientation the sample is carefully embedded prior to the experiment. However, after embedding the sample’s orientation is fixed and cannot be adapted to the sample’s development during in vivo experiments or to another region of interest anymore. Jahr 2015
