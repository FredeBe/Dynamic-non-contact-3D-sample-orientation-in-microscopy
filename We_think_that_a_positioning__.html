We think that a positioning method of biological samples needs to fulfill the following criteria to add a value to microscopy techniques. The technique needs to add a degree of freedom, which was not accessible in the microscope to be able to visualize the sample from the optimal side. Typically microscopes are equipped with a xyz stage to position the sample in the focal plane of the objective but there is no control of the sample orientation. The positioning technique must not interfere with the imaging of the sample. The method should be automatable allowing the integration in any automated microscopy routine. Ultimately, the technique has to ensure the sample integrity.
